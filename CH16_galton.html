Interactive Galton Graph

Instructions: This workbook plots the data from Galton's study of the relationship between parent and child heights. In cells C5 and C6, enter the slope and y-intercept of a line that you think would fit the data well. Suggested values for the slope are between 0.5 and 1.5. A line with the input slope and y-intercept is graphed as an orange line on the plot. (If you can't see the orange line, you need to adjust the y-intercept value.) To predict the height of a child with a given average parent height, find the y-value of the line when the x-value is the given average parent height. BME 16.1 Integrating Questions guide you through an exploration of determining which line is the best fit to the data, including how the residual sum of squares in cell E7 is calculated.
